About

I diagnose data problems — retention cliffs, metric drift, cost concentration. Root cause, not symptoms.

I’ve always been the person who notices the odd pattern: the one number that doesn’t fit, the metric that moves a week before anything else does. Data analysis just gave that habit a job description.

What I’ve actually learned in 3+ years:

  • The hardest part isn’t the SQL. It’s tracing a weird number back to its cause — and saying what it means in a sentence someone non-technical can act on.
  • At Cognizant, I traced report discrepancies to Azure Synapse refresh-cadence bugs. Not a data problem — an infrastructure one.
  • At Texas State, I turned a gut-feeling “we’re losing students” into an 18% drop-off pattern the department could plan around.
  • On 9.66M rows of Medicare claims, I found the 24 procedures driving 50% of $73B in spend — and modeled $801M in cost-reduction scenarios.
  • On 93K Olist customers, I diagnosed why their 3% repeat-rate had nothing to do with delivery speed and everything to do with category mix.

M.S. in Computer Science, Texas State University. I work in SQL, Python, Power BI, Tableau, and dbt — but the tools matter less than the habit: find what the numbers are trying to say, then say it plainly.
